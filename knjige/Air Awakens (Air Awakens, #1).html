    I'm going to answer this question…more    I just answered this question on my author page, but I'll answer it here also so that anyone/everyone can see it ---

    I'm going to answer this question completely honestly, because I want people to know what they're "getting into" with my books! But that means there are some very -very- general spoilers for the first book and the series. So, to anyone reading this, you've been warned ;)

    In the first book of the Air Awakens series there isn't any kissing, sex, etc. Not to say there isn't romance, because I'd say there's some definite attraction/tension between characters (I do call it a fantasy/romance book!). But, for reasons in the first book's story, it's not explored. However, it is worth noting that our heroine - Vhalla - is not a virgin and that is made clear in the first book.

    However, in later books there will be sexual themes and sex. This is part of why I say Air Awakens is intended for an older YA audience. Because while it will not be "explicit" like an Adult or New Adult novel, it will be present and will leave no questions to what's happening when it happens, to whatever characters it happens to.

    I realize sex in YA can be a touchy subject - which is why I like it when people ask me questions like this because I'm not of the inclination to get people to read something they'd have issues with. But, when I was writing Air Awakens I wanted to be as honest to the characters as I could be and if that meant sex at a point then I didn't want to shy from it, just as I don't want to shy from violence of the brutal natures of war and hate. I think books can play an important part in building a dialog surrounding these topics for young adults and so I decided to keep such scenes - as made sense - in the series.

    “Vhalla, you are a sorcerer.”

    Vhalla is a library apprentice whose world evolves around books and only books (stop staring at me mom), until one day she accidentally saves the crown prince's life and the Minister of Sorcery informs her that she is a sorcerer as well. While you or me would monkey dance at this prospect, Vhalla isn't as excited, since she and everyone in the South hates sorcerers and magic in general. Needless to say, Vhalla gradually comes around, with a little push (pun intended) from the crown prince who is also known as the Fire Lord (and everyone thinks of him as an arrogant bastard), and she discovers the potential but also the danger she faces as the last Windwalker.
    “Fire needs air to live. Air fuels fire, stokes it, and makes it burn brighter and hotter than it ever could alone. But too much air will snuff it completely, just as too many flames will consume all the air. They are far greater than the sum of their parts together, but are equally as dangerous to each other's existence.”

    Elise Kova is hands down an extremely talented author. Even though she introduced us into a brave new world with its own geography, history, religion and magic system, everything was woven with precision by hands that knew exactly what they were doing. Her writing is flawless, she manages to capture your attention since the first chapter and make you invested in the story and the characters. I even tried to manipulate the air around me (it was a long shot I know but I had to try. Maybe I have another Affinity?) but alas! magic is not my thing, so I had to live vicariously through Vhalla and it felt good.
    “Because, Vhalla, you think and you watch, but you never do. You can read all the books in this library, be wiser than the master himself someday, and then you will die having never really done anything. You will have only ever lived through everyone else's experiences.”

    It is safe to say that Vhalla did the same thing. I didn't like her at first because she was really narrow-minded and throughout the book she was meek to the wrong people, but her character development is astonishing so yeah, I eventually grew fond of her. There was also a parade of intriguing characters that supported the story and perplexed things and made everything even more interesting. And then there was Aldrik.
    "I may be a prince," he said as his lips brushed her ear lightly. "But I would trade it all to be a common man, even if only for tonight."



    He and Vhalla had the typical I-want-to-punch-you-and-then-kiss-you relationship that had me drooling and giggling and blushing. I was mesmerized by him. He was not a good person, just a heartless and snobbish prince who didn't inspire love or affection to his subjects, but he was misunderstood and he barely showed that he had a soft side. Their romance was a flame so bright that consumed me whole, it combined angst, tension, jealousy and tenderness, it turned my insides into a volcano. Prince Aldrik Solaris, welcome to my book boyfriends short-list.

    1.) The pacing - Like I said, it was totally inconsistent. Other than what I've mentioned earlier, there were a few times when a weeks have gone by in a span of a page, and sometimes a single day takes a chapter. It could've worked, but there was just something rough about the transitions.

    2.) The convenience and predictability - Let's face it, this book was kind of one of those formula books where you're never really shocked about anything since you saw things coming seven chapters away. You're just so sure that the heroine isn't in any real danger, and that just takes away most of the excitement.

    3.) The love triangle - I just don't get the point in it. It was senseless, trivial, and just overall really annoying. Did it add to the story? Nope. Did it give me any additional feels? Heck no. Did it serve a greater purpose for the plot development? Ha. No.

    4.) The minor characters - They were INCONSISTENT AS HECK. ***minor spoilers for this section***

    Exhibit A: Sareem, Vhalla's close friend and one side of the love triangle. See, at first I was ALL for Team Sareem (hey, that rhymed), but then when he discovered a few of the secrets she kept, he exploded and turned into a complete asshole. AND THEN a few pages later he's back to being a "good friend." Kay.

    Exhibit B: Roan, another one of Vhalla's "friends." She's totally protective and caring about the first, but when she found out about all the Sareem/Vhalla business, she went on bitch mode, which sucks because, hey, what happened to sisters before misters?

    THINGS I LIKED

    1.) The character growth - Okay, so, I didn't LOVE Vhalla at first--she was okay, mostly just your regular Special Snowflake heroine (you know, the kind who were really weak but were still labeled as "The One"), but she turned into a more believable heroine as the story flew by. She wasn't unbelievably badass or unbelievably petty--she was just your average, relatable heroine, who was smart, open-minded and kind.

    2.) The romance... at some point - AT FIRST the romance was a tad too fast paced and had no chemistry, but HOLY CRAP by the last 3/4ths of the book, I was just exploding with feels for a certain ship in this book.

    3.) The ending - This book ended so strongly that I had to give this book 2.5 stars despite disliking so many things about it. There was a brilliant twist, intense action and a lot of things going on. It's the kind of ending that'll leave you wanting a lot more.

    CONCLUSION

    If you've read and enjoyed the more popular YA high fantasy novels out there, like Sarah J. Maas's Throne of Glass, you might find yourself enjoying Air Awakens as well. But if you think you're not ready to dig into another cookie cutter YA, this might not be the book for you.

    “I will tell you one more thing.” The master paused at the door. “The prisoner told me that it was a shame the East and West could not have worked together. He said, ‘Fire needs air to live. Air fuels fire, stokes it, and makes it burn brighter and hotter than it ever could alone. But too much air will snuff it completely, just as too many flames will consume all the air. They are far greater than the sum of their parts together, but are equally as dangerous to each other’s existence.’”

    Few days ago, I have seen the review of my friend Katerina Kondrenko (which I highly recommend) and I found this book everything that I needed right now. Best of all: it didn't disappoint me and my expectations were met. I am so glad to say that because after reading for a while, I was kind of worried if I will like it in the end. But, considering how it made me cry at the end of the book, I think the book was done pretty well.

    At first there was a lot of info-dump, but it is not so hard to follow - it took me a while to remember it all, but it was in fact quite simple. Remember Avatar the Last Airbender? The idea of this book is pretty much similar. Air, Earth, Fire, Water - each power connected to geographic region, an Emperor who wants to conquer the world and Air sorcerers extinct? This is a problem I had with this book - it was similar to avatar, but even so it was not the same and the characters made up for it. It's quite possible you will love it even if you watched ATLA before. This is why I had to lower the rating for 0.5 stars. I would have lowered more for it, but I enjoyed it too much to do it. And a plus side: writing was wonderful and there were many quotes I highlighted.

    While world building and certain cliches may not be the strong side of the book yet, the characters made up for it. The book is written in third person POV, but only one POV - Vhalla's. Even though I wished for Prince Aldrik to get a POV too, I think it was well done without his own POV, because it made you wonder about his true character. Out of all people, he was the hardest nut to crack.

    So basically, this book IS focused on relationships between the characters and their feelings than on the story itself.

    ► WORLD BUILDING:


    World building is quite simple, even though it's not quite original.

    Solaris Empire's ruler wants to conquer the world and unify it under one rule. The only side of the world left standing is North - against which the Empire wars against.

    Magic system is based on elements: Fire, Water, Earth and Air. "Firebearers are from the West, Waterrunners from the South, and Groundbreakers from the North." And then, there is East... East is a homeland of Air sorcerers - but in the last 150 years they were killed or enslaved for their power and went almost extinct (Sadly, another parallel with ATLA). Each of the sorcerers wields only one of the elements, but it's not strictly only one power, but branches of one element - for example, Waterrunners can freeze.

    Sorcery is a theme of talk in the Empire - "ordinary" people find sorcery frightening and repulsive. But the Empire has a Tower of Sorcerers, and a crown Prince, the oldest one, Aldrik, also a commandant of Black Legion, who is a Firebearer. And even so, the Tower Sorcerers are feared and rejected everywhere and by everyone, even though they are a force that is vital in the wars. Not everyone has magic, and it is not passed genetically - the sorcerer's powers manifest through time and one can choose to be Eradicated - remove the powers - or join the Tower.

    ► STORY:


    An ordinary Library apprentice Vhalla Yarl wants nothing more than the life full of books and the work she enjoys doing. She has friends, everything she needs, everything she wants and desires.

    When one day one of the princes returned from the battlefield, poisoned by a blade, she believes it was Prince Baldair, the one she met in the Library. Therefore, she writes notes on everything she found about the poison and thankfully saved his life. But... it is not Prince Baldair she saved, but his older brother, crown prince and sorcerer of the Tower, Aldrik himself. Aldrik's reputation is quite severe and she is - like everyone else - afraid of him. But she didn't expect that in the process of saving his life, she would start manifesting magic, and that Aldrik would find it out because it was her magic that saved his life.

    Vhalla is literally and forcefully thrown in the world of magic by Aldrik and the consequence of her Awakening ended up as traumatizing event (view spoiler)[ throwing her off a roof is not a great way to start friendship, Aldrik... (hide spoiler)].

    “Aren’t you lucky to be a prince and not have your mistakes have consequences?” Vhalla remarked sharply. “They do,” he responded quickly and firmly. “The consequence was the loss of your trust.”


    Vhalla is struggling with the choice she is given: should she accept the magic and pain she believes it brings, or remove it from her life and live an ordinary life again?

    ► CHARACTERS:


    ☀ Vhalla Yarl is the protagonist of the book. She is an ordinary, almost 18 years old girl who has had relationships before, who wants to live an ordinary life filled with books and have fun with her friends Sareem and Roan. But her life changes the moment she manifests her magic by helping a prince she is not really fond of. Vhalla wants to know, to learn, of things, and even though she was scared of sorcerers, she finds herself wondering about their magic, their ways and why they are so feared. But the breach of trust she experienced forces her to rethink about everything she has ever known. If she should give up what she has or go in the Tower, have magic and abandon everything familiar to her?

    {Vhalla is curious and a seeker of knowledge. She wants to know the truth, she wants honesty and trust and she is generally nice and kind person. As well as sensitive - which is quite understandable after everything she goes through in such a short time. Even though Vhalla annoyed me from time to time, she was also interesting and loveable character with much potential.}

    ☀ Prince Aldrik , crown prince, leader of Black Legion (the army of sorcerers) and Firebearer, is a man feared by everyone. He has a reputation that he himself believes it was well earned. To everyone he seems like a cold, calculating person devoid of emotion, selfish, egoistical, snarky, silver tongued and narrow minded. But what people don't know is that he tries so hard to do the right thing, he ends up doing the wrong thing instead - which only makes the matters worse. He made mistakes - and he is not afraid to admit them, but sometimes those mistakes hurt him as much as they hurt the other people.

    {For the first 25% of the book, I didn't really like Aldrik, he was everything I described above and the first thing that went through my mind was how he was going to sort this out. He is what he seems and in the same time quite different. There are many things he shows through the book: but is he faking it or is a truth? Through the whole book it is a bit hard to determine what his true personality is: is he a manipulator? A puppeteer? Cruel? Self-centered and self-absorbed person? Awkward? Kind? Thoughtful? Liar? Reckless? A good man? But one thing is for sure: he is a person with a lot of enemies. What I loved about Aldrik was his complexity - I love when characters are complicated even if they are hero or anti-hero type.}

    ☀ Prince Baldair is the second son of the Emperor and he leads an army. He is everything Aldrik is not... a complete opposite of him. While Aldrik inspires fear and caution and unwelcome atmosphere, Baldair radiates trust, light and inspiration to his own troops and people. But prince Baldair is known to be heartbreaker, due to his bright personality and seducing nature. But then one has to ask: is he faking kindness, is he faking everything?

    ☀ Roan and Sareem are Vhalla's friends from the Library and the ones she can confide in. But Sareem has been crushing on Vhalla for a long, long time, but he doesn't know how to approach Vhalla because Vhalla doesn't realize it... yet.

    ☀ Minister Victor & Larel and Fritz from the tower want to help Vhalla with her dilemmas and troubles. Both of them want her to choose magic and to be here with them. Victor and Larel are both kind and thoughtful and want to help as much as possible. Larel is also one of the true friends Aldrik has. Fritz is the librarian from the Tower who helps Vhalla and becomes her friend. He is hyperactive, cheerful and lovely guy who is also in relationship with Grahm, another sorcerer.

    ☀ There are, of course, more characters, like Senator Egmun and Master Mohned, Emperor himself, and the others. But I don't want to give away too much.

    Despite what I wrote, even though it looks like there could be love n-angles, it's not really present. One of them is completely one sided. And it was done well and solved quickly, so there is no worries there. What I meant to say is that there was no focus on love triangles, which was nice.
